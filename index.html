World Animals

Foxes

Foxes are mammalian and omnivorous animals belonging to the Canidae family. They are medium-sized vulpids, characterized by a long snout and a long, bushy tail.

They also feature their oval pupils, similar to the vertical pupils of felines.

Squirrels

Squirrels are considered one of the largest rodents in the world. In this way, they can measure up to 45 centimeters, much of this due to their long tail. In addition, they have very large eyes and teeth that stand out in the head region.

These rodents feed on a large number of plants and seeds, including nuts. All the food they receive is usually stored in their safe place. The latter is something very common when starting its hibernation phase.

Something unique about these animals is their agility to move through the trees. In this way, they manage to climb them from an early age and manage to have fingers that help them carry out all kinds of daily activities.

The life expectancy of Squirrels is on average 6 to 10 years, as long as we count that they have not suffered from any illness and that they have had a full life, full of food, without lacking anything.

Bear

Bears are fascinating and imposing animals that inhabit diverse ecosystems around the world. They are important to nature, playing key roles in maintaining the ecological balance and in the food chain.

Their diet and activity change according to the habitat where they are inserted, as well as the season of the year.

Bears in general are considered “opportunists”, since they eat what is available: from fruits to other animals. Despite being found in drier regions, species of the Ursidae family need contact with water and are more abundant in temperate regions.

Wolf

Wolves live in groups, packs, led by an alpha male, which has greater strength and hunting power compared to other members of the group.

The alpha male forms a couple with the alpha female, responsible for the females of the pack. They form a monogamous couple who live together all their lives and lead the pack.

When an alpha male dies, leadership of the pack passes to the beta male, second in the pack's organizational hierarchy.

In general, wolves are intelligent, fast and strategists when hunting, being recognized as excellent predators.

Monkey

A monkey is any homonoid (member of the superfamily Homonoidae). This includes the "lesser apes", 13 species of gibbon, which live in the tropical and subtropical forests of Southeast Asia, and the "great apes", family Homonidae, which includes orangutans, gorillas, chimpanzees and humans, all of whom are closely related.

TAll monkeys are omnivores, consuming fruits, grass seeds and the occasional insect or small animal.

Most are social who traditionally live in groups of up to about 200 members, although cities can be arbitrarily large.

Lion

The lion is a large feline mammal, vertebrate and carnivore. Its scientific name is Panthera leo.

Known as the King of the Jungle, the lion can be found in Asia, Africa and Europe.

It is currently classified as vulnerable to extinction by the International Union for Conservation of Nature and Natural Resources (IUCN).
